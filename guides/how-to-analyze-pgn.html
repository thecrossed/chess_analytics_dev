How to Analyze PGN with Stockfish

This workflow helps you turn engine output into training decisions, not just numbers.

1. Prepare the PGN

Use a complete PGN with move list and result. If possible, include headers like event date and player names so your future review stays organized.

2. Run analysis at practical depth

Start around depth 12-15 for full-game scan. Re-run only critical moves at depth 18-20. This gives you speed first and precision where it matters.

3. Read eval trend, not isolated numbers

Track where evaluation changes sharply after a move. Big jumps often indicate tactical misses, blunders, or strategic turning points.

4. Compare played move vs best move

Use bestmove and bestmove_eval to understand what stronger alternative existed in the same position. This is where concrete improvement comes from.

5. Build a short training list

After each game, keep 3-5 key mistakes and label them by theme: opening plan, tactical calculation, time management, or endgame conversion.
